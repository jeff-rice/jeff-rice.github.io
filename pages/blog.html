Bowling in the Fast Lane: Navigating the Ever-Changing World of Bowling Tech

Written by: Jeff Rice - USBC Bronze Level Coach
Published: Jan 13, 2023

In the dynamic world of bowling, keeping pace with technological advancements can be quite a challenge. It's like trying to catch a speeding train - new developments are constantly emerging. Imagine this: almost every month, we're seeing a fresh batch of bowling balls from various manufacturers. There's more - changes in the oil used on lanes, innovative gear for better ball release, wrist support, and perfecting the slide. It's a whirlwind of innovation, and you might wonder, "When does it all stop?"

Well, here's the scoop: it doesn't. The bowling arena is evolving, getting fiercely competitive by the year. Take the two-handed bowling style, for example. It's reshaping the game, and ball companies are hustling to adapt. Is this style superior? That's a debate for another day. A recent piece in 'The Bowler's Journal' showcased four new balls as of January 2023: Roto Grip Exotic Gem, Motiv Jackal Ambush, Storm Revenant, and Storm Absolute.

Each ball offers something unique, but here's the kicker - not every ball is a one-size-fits-all solution. I, for instance, am a Hammer bowling ball enthusiast, mainly because that's my comfort zone. My daughter, on the other hand, swears by Storm balls. The point is, with technology racing ahead, you've got to do some digging to find what clicks for you. The best tip I can offer? Head over to your local Pro Shop at the bowling center. Strike up a chat with the experts there. Be upfront about what you're looking for, and don't shy away from honesty. Many bowlers think they've got their needs all figured out, but often, they might not fully grasp their own game. And that's okay! Your pro shop professional is there to guide you.

Striking Out the 10-Pin Puzzle: A Bowlers's Guide to Smart Adjustments

Written by: Jeff Rice - USBC Bronze Level Coach
Published: Jan 13, 2023

Ever rolled what you thought was a perfect shot, only to be greeted by that stubborn 10-pin (or 7-pin for lefties) standing tall? If you're nodding in agreement, this one's for you. We've all been there, throwing what feels like great balls, but then - wham! - a relentless sequence of 10-pins mocks us.

The 10-pin, or its left-handed counterpart, the 7-pin, is perhaps the most frustrating outcome of what seemed like a spot-on shot. It's so notorious, it might as well be called the "@#$%! 10-pin". In the world of bowling, there are two main culprits: the 'ringing 10-pin,' where a pin (often the 6-pin) circles the 10-pin instead of knocking it down, and the 'flat 10-pin,' where a pin (yes, usually the 6-pin again) just lies in the gutter, neglecting its duty to topple the 10-pin.

Now, faced with this scenario, what's your move? Shift left, right, forward, backward? These questions plague bowlers, along with a common myth: the causes of flat and ringing 10-pins are distinct, with the ringing 10 being the result of a "better" shot. But, as Freeman and Hatfield reveal in 'Bowling Beyond the Basics' (2018), both these 10-pin scenarios stem from a light hit. Surprisingly, the smart move is often a shift right, countering the usual instinct to move left. This revelation was an eye-opener for me too. I used to think a sharp angle into the pocket was to blame, but after putting this theory to the test, I found moving right significantly reduced my 10-pin leaves.

So, next time you're in league play, wrestling with 10-pins, try sliding a board right and see what unfolds. You might just walk away less frustrated and more triumphant.

Striking Success: Your Guide to Becoming a Better Bowler

Written by: Jeff Rice - USBC Bronze Level Coach
Published: December 4, 2023

You've probably noticed a bunch of folks on social media asking the same question: "How do I level up my bowling game?" It's a common query, and you'll get a different answer depending on who you ask. But before we dive into the nitty-gritty, let's nail down what you're really after.

I could tell you to just get better at throwing the ball, or I could suggest dropping a grand on all-new bowling gear, hoping for a miracle improvement. But honestly, it doesn't quite work like that.

First off, let's get one thing straight: becoming a better bowler doesn't happen overnight. It's like any sport; it takes a ton of practice and repetition. Depending on where you're starting, you might even see your scores dip and feel a bit lost before you start seeing real progress. It's part of the game.

You'll hit roadblocks and moments when you're like, "What am I doing?" But remember what the great Michael Jordan once said,

"Obstacles don't have to stop you. If you run into a wall, don't turn around and give up. Figure out how to climb it, go through it, or work around it."

Now, back to the main question: How do you kickstart this journey of improvement? Here are a few key things to set you on the right path:

  • Get a Coach Who Knows the Game:
    • Seriously, a good coach can make a world of difference. They've got the experience and tricks up their sleeves to give you personalized advice and help you tackle those bowling challenges.
  • Chat Up the Pro Shop Pros:
    • Pay a visit to your local bowling alley's pro shop and have a chat with the experts there. They'll hook you up with the right gear that suits your style and preferences. Having the right equipment is a game-changer.
  • Be Ready to Learn and Practice... A Lot:
    • Improvement is all about practice and learning from your slip-ups. So, be prepared to pu in the hours, practice religiously, and keep at it consistently.
  • Above all... Enjoy the Experience:
    • Bowling is a fun social activity, so relax and enjoy the process. Focus on having a good time with friends and family and when you are ready to become more competitive, have patience while you get there.

In a nutshell, the journey to becoming a better bowler isn't a quick fix or a shortcut. It's a trek that requires commitment, some pro guidance, and a hefty dose of self-improvement. So, lace up those bowling shoes, grab your ball, and let's roll into this adventure towards bowling greatness!
